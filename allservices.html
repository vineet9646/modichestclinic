Our Services

Which Services We Provide

Allergy Blood Test

A blood allergy test is a diagnostic method used to identify allergies by measuring the presence of specific immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies in the blood. Blood allergy tests are particularly useful in cases where skin testing is not feasible or when a patient cannot stop taking antihistamines.

Digital X Ray

Digital X-ray, also known as digital radiography, is a modern medical imaging technique that uses digital sensors instead of traditional photographic film to capture and create diagnostic images. It has largely replaced conventional X-ray film technology in many healthcare settings due to its numerous advantages.

12 Channnel ECG

A 12-lead electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG) is a diagnostic test that measures the electrical activity of the heart. The "12 channels" refer to the different views of the heart's electrical activity that the ECG records. Each lead represents a different viewpoint, providing a comprehensive assessment of the heart's functioning.

Spirometer/PFT

Spirometry is a common diagnostic test used to assess lung function. It measures the volume and flow of air that a person can inhale and exhale. Spirometry is a non- invasive procedure and is typically performed by a healthcare professional, such as a respiratory therapist or pulmonologist.

FeNO Test for Asthma

FeNO stands for Fractional Exhaled Nitric Oxide. The FeNO test is a non-invasive and simple diagnostic tool used in respiratory medicine to measure the level of nitric oxide in a person's breath. Nitric oxide is a gas produced by cells in the lining of the airways, and its levels can increase in response to inflammation, particularly in conditions such as asthma.

Fibro Scan for Liver

A FibroScan device uses a technology called transient elastography to measure the stiffness of the liver. It's particularly useful in evaluating liver fibrosis, which is the scarring of liver tissue that can result from various liver diseases, such as hepatitis B and C, alcoholic liver disease, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), and others.

Skin Allergy Test

It can help determine specific substances to which a person may be allergic. Allergy tests work by detecting the presence of specific IgE antibodies that the immune system produces in response to allergens. When the allergen is introduced into the skin, it can trigger an allergic reaction in individuals sensitized to that particular substance.

Sleep Study Test

A sleep study, or polysomnography (PSG), diagnoses sleep disorders by monitoring physiological parameters during sleep, such as breathing, brain activity, heart rate, and movements. Conducted in sleep centers, it helps diagnose conditions like sleep apnea, insomnia, and narcolepsy, providing insights for effective treatment plans to improve overall well-being.

FOT/Impulse Oscillometer

The IOS is a device for testing, assessing the respiratory system, focusing on airways and lungs. By delivering pressure pulses to the airway, it measures impedance, compliance, and other respiratory parameters. Pulmonologists and respiratory therapists use IOS to evaluate lung function, especially for detailed peripheral airways information in diagnosis and treatment planning.

Flexible Bronchoscopy

Bronchoscopy, a medical procedure, examines airways and lungs through a flexible or rigid bronchoscope inserted via the nose or mouth. This allows direct visualization of bronchial tubes and lung tissue. Two main types of bronchoscopes, flexible and rigid, serve for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes in assessing respiratory conditions and guiding treatments.

From common cold remedies to navigating new prescriptions, we offer guidance and resources. Stay informed and make confident decisions with our expert advice, ensuring a seamless healthcare experience for you and your loved ones.

Committed to enhancing community health, we deliver timely, accurate, and cost-effective testing services, ensuring the well-being of our community.
